# Cyber Laws & Legal Framework — India

This document lists key `Indian cyber laws`, sections, and regulations relevant to
digital forensics, cybercrime investigation, and `cyber policing activities`.



***1. Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act):***

Primary legislation governing cybercrime and electronic evidence in India.

**Important Sections:**

- Section 43 – Unauthorized access, data damage
- Section 65 – Tampering with computer source documents
- Section 66 – Computer-related offences
- Section 66C – Identity theft
- Section 66D – Cheating by personation using computer resources
- Section 66E – Violation of privacy
- Section 67 – Publishing obscene content
- Section 67A – Sexually explicit content
- Section 67B – Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)



***2. Indian Penal Code (IPC) — Cyber Relevance:***

Certain IPC sections are used along with IT Act:

- IPC 419 – Cheating by impersonation
- IPC 420 – Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property
- IPC 463–471 – Forgery & use of forged documents
- IPC 354D – Cyber stalking
- IPC 500 – Defamation (online cases)



***3. Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC):***

- Section 91 – Summons to produce electronic records
- Section 100 – Search & seizure
- Section 165 – Police search during investigation



***4. Indian Evidence Act, 1872:***

**Electronic Evidence:**
- Section 65A – Special provisions for electronic evidence
- Section 65B – Admissibility of electronic evidence
  (Hash values, integrity & authenticity are critical)



***5. CERT-In Directions (2022):***

Issued under IT Act Section 70B:
- Mandatory incident reporting
- Log retention (180 days)
- Time-bound breach disclosure



***6. Digital Evidence Principles (India):***

- Evidence must be collected legally
- No alteration or tampering
- Proper chain of custody
- Documentation at every stage
